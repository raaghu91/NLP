Pronunciation[edit]  (US) IPA(key): /ˈdaʊnˌraɪt/  Adjective[edit] downright (comparative more downright, superlative most downright)  Directed vertically; coming straight down. Directly to the point; plain; unambiguous; unevasive. Using plain direct language; accustomed to express opinions directly and bluntly; blunt. Complete; absolute; utter.  Synonyms[edit]  evendown  Derived terms[edit]  downrightness  Adverb[edit] downright (not comparable)  Really; actually; quite; thoroughly; utterly.  He wasn't just cool to me, he was downright rude.   Straight down; perpendicularly. Without delay; at once.  Arbuthnot  She fell downright into a fit.      Usage notes[edit] "Downright" is used to intensify or emphasize the following adjective, which usually refers to some negative quality.
