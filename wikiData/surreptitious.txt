   Contents   1 English  1.1 Pronunciation 1.2 Etymology 1.3 Adjective  1.3.1 Synonyms 1.3.2 Derived terms 1.3.3 Translations        English[edit] Pronunciation[edit]  (US) IPA(key): /ˌsɜɹ.əpˈtɪʃ.əs/  Etymology[edit] From Latin surrepticius (“stolen, furtive, clandestine”), from surreptus, past participle of surripere ("seize secretly, steal, pilfer"), from sub- ("under") + rapere ("to snatch"). Adjective[edit] surreptitious (comparative more surreptitious, superlative most surreptitious)  Stealthy, furtive, well hidden, covert (especially movements).   1922,  Ben Travers,  chapter 1, A Cuckoo in the Nest[1]:  He read the letter aloud. Sophia listened with the studied air of one for whom, even in these days, a title possessed some surreptitious allurement.
