(file)        Rhymes: -æbəl    Etymology[edit] From dab Verb[edit] dabble (third-person singular simple present dabbles, present participle dabbling, simple past and past participle dabbled)  (transitive) To partially wet (something) by splashing or dipping; connotes playfulness.  The children sat on the dock and dabbled their feet in the water.   (intransitive) To participate or have an interest in an activity, but in a casual or superficial way.  She's an actress by trade, but has been known to dabble in poetry.
