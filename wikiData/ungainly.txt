   Contents   1 English  1.1 Etymology 1.2 Adjective  1.2.1 Translations        English[edit] Etymology[edit] From un- + Old Norse gegn (“fit, honest”) + -ly. Adjective[edit] ungainly (comparative ungainlier, superlative ungainliest)  clumsy; lacking grace.  Macaulay  His ungainly figure and eccentric manners.     difficult to move or to manage; unwieldy. (obsolete) unsuitable; unprofitable  (Can we find and add a quotation of Hammond to this entry?)
