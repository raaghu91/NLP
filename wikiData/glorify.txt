   Contents   1 English  1.1 Etymology 1.2 Verb  1.2.1 Translations        English[edit] Etymology[edit] From Middle English glorifien, from Anglo-Norman glorifier, from Old French, from Late Latin glorificō, from Latin gloria + faciō (“to make”). Verb[edit] glorify (third-person singular simple present glorifies, present participle glorifying, simple past and past participle glorified)  (transitive) to exalt, or give glory or praise to (something or someone) (transitive) to make (something) appear to be more glorious than it is (transitive) to worship or extol  Translations[edit]  to exalt, or give glory or praise to something or someone      Faroese: æra Finnish: ylistää (fi) French: glorifier (fr) German: verherrlichen (de)      Luxembourgish: verherrlechen Maori: whakakorōria Russian: прославлять (ru) impf (proslavljátʹ), прославить (ru) pf (proslávitʹ), восхвалять (ru) impf (vosxvaljátʹ), восхвалить (ru) pf (vosxvalítʹ) Volapük: glorükön (vo)        to make something appear to be more glorious than it is      Finnish: kaunistella German: glorifizieren      Romanian: glorifica (ro) Russian: превозносить (ru) impf (prevoznosítʹ), превознести (ru) pf (prevoznestí)        to worship or extol      Finnish: palvoa (fi) German: anbeten (de)      Russian: боготворить (ru) impf (bogotvorítʹ), поклоняться (ru) impf (poklonjátʹsja) Volapük: Godiglorükön
