A jacket is a mid stomach length garment for the upper body. A jacket typically has sleeves, and fastens in the front or slightly on the side. A jacket is generally lighter, tighter-fitting, and less insulating than a coat, which is outerwear. Some jackets are fashionable, while others serve as protective clothing. The term comes from the Middle French noun jaquet, which refers to a small or lightweight tunic.[1] In Modern French, jaquette is synonymous with jacket.
