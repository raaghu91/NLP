WOTD – 19 August 2006    Contents   1 English  1.1 Etymology 1.2 Pronunciation 1.3 Adjective  1.3.1 Related terms 1.3.2 Translations   1.4 Anagrams      English[edit] Etymology[edit] From Middle English irkesome, irksum, equivalent to irk +‎ -some, or from Old English weorcsum (“painful, hurtful”). Pronunciation[edit]  enPR: ûrk'səm, IPA(key): /ˈɚksəm/  Adjective[edit] irksome (comparative more irksome, superlative most irksome)  Disagreeable or troublesome by reason of long continuance or repetition; bothersome; annoying; irritating; wearisome; tedious.  He has this irksome habit of racing up to red lights, so he has to brake heavily.
