   Contents   1 English  1.1 Alternative forms 1.2 Etymology 1.3 Pronunciation 1.4 Verb  1.4.1 Synonyms   1.5 Anagrams      English[edit] Alternative forms[edit]  indear  Etymology[edit] From en- +‎ dear. Pronunciation[edit]  (UK) IPA(key): /ɪnˈdɪə/, /ɛnˈdɪə/ (US) IPA(key): /ɪnˈdɪəɹ/ Rhymes: -ɪə(r)  Verb[edit] endear (third-person singular simple present endears, present participle endearing, simple past and past participle endeared)  (obsolete) To make (something) more precious or valuable. [16th-17th c.] (obsolete) To make (something) more expensive; to increase the cost of. [17th-19th c.] (obsolete) To stress (something) as important; to exaggerate. [17th c.]  1603, John Florio, translating Michel de Montaigne, Essays, II.18:  Salvianus Massiliensis […] saith, that amongst French-men, to lie and forsweare is no vice but a manner of speach. He that would endeare [transl. encherir] this Testimonie, might say, it is now rather deemed a vertue among them.     To make (someone) dear or precious.
