(file)    Rhymes: -eɪk Hyphenation: par‧take  Verb[edit] partake (third-person singular simple present partakes, present participle partaking, simple past partook, past participle partaken)  (intransitive, formal) To take part in an activity; to participate.  John Locke  Brutes partake in this faculty.     (intransitive) To take a share or portion (of).  Will you partake of some food?   (intransitive, obsolete) To have something of the properties, character, or office (of).  Francis Bacon  The attorney of the Duchy of Lancaster partakes partly of a judge, and partly of an attorney-general.
