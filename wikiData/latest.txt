Laporte editor, Simple curiosity; letters from George Gaylord Simpson ..., published 1987,  page 29:  And like other futile edifices of man these are inhabited for a brief space giving glory to the proprietor of the most unusual or striking and then left to melt back to dust and be forgotten, or worse yet, to become curiosities for generations with other "latests".     1979,  Edward Digby Baltzell,  Puritan Boston and Quaker Philadelphia,  page 54:  It has often been said that Philadelphia is the city of firsts, Boston of bests, and New York of latests.
