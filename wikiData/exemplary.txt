   Contents   1 English  1.1 Etymology 1.2 Adjective  1.2.1 Related terms 1.2.2 Translations   1.3 Noun      English[edit] Etymology[edit] From Middle French exemplaire, from Latin exemplaris, from exemplum. Adjective[edit] exemplary (comparative more exemplary, superlative most exemplary)  Deserving honour, respect and admiration. Of such high quality that it should serve as an example to be imitated.  Francis Bacon  [Bishops'] lives and doctrines ought to be exemplary.     Ideal or perfect.
