   Contents   1 English  1.1 Alternative forms 1.2 Etymology 1.3 Pronunciation 1.4 Noun 1.5 Verb      English[edit] Alternative forms[edit]  prævision (obsolete)  Etymology[edit] From French prévision, from Old French, from Latin praevisio. Pronunciation[edit]  (UK) IPA(key): /pɹɪˈvɪʒ(ə)n/  Noun[edit] prevision (plural previsions)  Advance knowledge; foresight.  1897, Henry James, What Maisie Knew:  it was the beginning for her of a deeper prevision that, in spite of Miss Overmore's brilliancy and Mrs. Wix's passion, she should live to see a change in the nature of the struggle she appeared to have come into the world to produce.   1946, Bertrand Russell, History of Western Philosophy, I.21:  The whole discussion is concerned with City States, and their is no prevision of their obsolescence.     A prediction.  Verb[edit] prevision (third-person singular simple present previsions, present participle previsioning, simple past and past participle previsioned)  To predict or envision the future.
