   Contents   1 English  1.1 Alternative forms 1.2 Etymology 1.3 Pronunciation 1.4 Verb  1.4.1 Synonyms 1.4.2 Translations        English[edit] Alternative forms[edit]  patronise (Commonwealth)  Etymology[edit] Old English patron (reborrowed from Latin patronus, derived from Latin pater (“father”)) +‎ -ize (“(verb ending)”). Pronunciation[edit]  IPA(key): /ˈpeɪt(ʃ).ɹə.naɪz/, /ˈpæt(ʃ).ɹə.naɪz/  Verb[edit] patronize (third-person singular simple present patronizes, present participle patronizing, simple past and past participle patronized)  (transitive) To make a patron. (transitive) To assume a tone of unjustified superiority; to talk down to; to treat condescendingly. (transitive) To make oneself a customer of a business, especially a regular customer.
