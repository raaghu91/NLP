   Contents   1 English  1.1 Etymology 1.2 Pronunciation 1.3 Noun  1.3.1 Related terms 1.3.2 Translations        English[edit] Etymology[edit] From Middle French instigation, from Latin instigatio Pronunciation[edit]  Rhymes: -eɪʃən  Noun[edit] instigation (plural instigations)  The act of instigating, or the state of being instigated; incitement; especially to evil or wickedness.   1922,  Ben Travers,  chapter 5, A Cuckoo in the Nest:  The departure was not unduly prolonged. […] Within the door Mrs. Spoker hastily imparted to Mrs. Love a few final sentiments on the subject of Divine Intention in the disposition of buckets; farewells and last commiserations; a deep, guttural instigation to the horse; and the wheels of the waggonette crunched heavily away into obscurity.
