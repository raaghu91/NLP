   Contents   1 English  1.1 Etymology 1.2 Pronunciation 1.3 Adjective  1.3.1 Synonyms 1.3.2 Derived terms 1.3.3 Translations        English[edit] Etymology[edit] From French désastreux, from Middle French desastre (modern désastre) 'disaster', from Italian disastro, itself from dis- "away, without" (from Latin) + astro "star, planet" (from Latin astrum 'star, celestial body', from Ancient Greek (astron)). Pronunciation[edit]  (UK) IPA(key): /dɪˈzɑːstɹəs/  Adjective[edit] disastrous (comparative more disastrous, superlative most disastrous)  of the nature of a disaster; calamitous. foreboding disaster; ill-omened.  Synonyms[edit]  (calamitous): cataclysmic, catastrophic (ill-omened): ill-boding, inausîcuous, sinister  Derived terms[edit]  disastrously  Translations[edit]  of the nature of a disaster; calamitous      Bulgarian: бедствен (bg) (bedstven), катастрофален (bg) (katastrofalen) Catalan: desastrós Dutch: rampzalig (nl), catastrofaal (nl), rampspoedig French: désastreux (fr) German: desaströs (de), katastrophal (de), verheerend (de) Hungarian: súlyos (hu), katasztrofális (hu) Japanese: 忌まわしい (imawashii) Korean: 재난의 (ko) (jae-nan-ui), 처참하다 (cheocham-hada)      Portuguese: desastroso (pt) Russian: бе́дственный (ru) (bédstvennyj), ги́бельный (ru) (gíbelʹnyj), катастрофи́ческий (ru) (katastrofíčeskij) Serbo-Croatian:  Cyrillic: ка̏тастрофа̄лан m Roman: kȁtastrofālan m   Spanish: desastroso Swedish: ödesdiger (sv), katastrofal (sv) Turkish: feci (tr)        foreboding disaster; ill-omened      Bulgarian: пагубен (bg) (paguben) Dutch: onheilspellend, sinister (nl) German: verhängnisvoll      Hungarian: baljós (hu) Japanese: 忌まわしい (imawashii) Russian: па́губный (ru) (págubnyj)         The translations below need to be checked and inserted above into the appropriate translation tables, removing any numbers.
