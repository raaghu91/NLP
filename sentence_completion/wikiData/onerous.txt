Pronunciation[edit]  (UK) IPA(key): /ˈəʊn.ɜː(ɹ).ʌs/  Adjective[edit] onerous (comparative more onerous, superlative most onerous)  imposing or constituting a physical, mental, or figurative load which can be borne only with effort.  1820, Washington Irving, "The Legend of Sleepy Hollow":  That all this might not be too onerous on the purses of his rustic patrons, who are apt to consider the costs of schooling a grievous burden, and schoolmasters as mere drones, he had various ways of rendering himself both useful and agreeable.   1848, Charlotte Brontë, Shirley, ch. 13:  Again, and more intensely than ever, she desired a fixed occupation,—no matter how onerous, how irksome.   1910, Jack London, "The Golden Poppy" in Revolution and Other Essays:  [I]t has become an onerous duty, a wearisome and distasteful task.
