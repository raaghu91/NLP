   Contents   1 English  1.1 Etymology 1.2 Noun  1.2.1 Translations   1.3 Anagrams      English[edit] Etymology[edit] From Middle French partialité, from Medieval Latin partialitas Noun[edit] partiality (plural partialities)  Preference, bias in favor of, tendency.  The judge's partiality towards the defendant caused him to be replaced, with someone who was apparently more neutral.
