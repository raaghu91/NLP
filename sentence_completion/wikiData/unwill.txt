   Contents   1 English  1.1 Etymology 1  1.1.1 Noun   1.2 Etymology 2  1.2.1 Verb        English[edit] Etymology 1[edit] From un- (“lack or absence of”) +‎ will (noun). Noun[edit] unwill (plural unwills)  Lack or absence of will; willlessness; undesire.  2005, Melodie Calvert, Jennifer Terry, Processed Lives:  The first challenge to shaping and taming this emerging world is the will itself and the human problem of unwill, especially in relation to femininity.      Etymology 2[edit] From un- (reverse action prefix) +‎ will (verb). Verb[edit] unwill (third-person singular simple present unwills, present participle unwilling, simple past and past participle unwilled)  (transitive) To annul or reverse by an act of the will.
