(file)    Rhymes: -aʊnd  Verb[edit] expound (third-person singular simple present expounds, present participle expounding, simple past and past participle expounded)  To lay open; to expose to view; to examine or exposit. To lay open the meaning of; to explain or discuss at length; to clear of obscurity; to interpret.   1891,  Oscar Wilde,  The Picture of Dorian Gray:  Some day, when you are tired of London, come down to Treadley, and expound to me your philosophy of pleasure over some admirable Burgundy I am fortunate enough to possess.
